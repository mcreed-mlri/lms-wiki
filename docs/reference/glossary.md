# Glossary

Key terms used in Brightspace and in this wiki. When someone on the team uses one of these terms, this is what they mean at MLRI.

---

## Brightspace Terms

### Course Offering
The actual learner-facing course — where content, assessments, discussions, completion tracking, and learning activities live. When you enroll a learner, you enroll them in an Offering. A Course Template may contain multiple Offerings (different versions over time).

**At MLRI:** This is what attorneys see and interact with. Name it cleanly — no internal jargon or version numbers visible to learners.

---

### Course Template
An organizational container that holds all versions of a course. Templates are not learner-facing — learners don't enroll in templates, they enroll in Offerings inside a template. Templates exist to keep version history organized without cluttering the main catalog.

**At MLRI:** Named `[Course Name] Template` (e.g., *Legal Writing Template*). Contains the current Offering and any ARCHIVED versions.

---

### Section
An optional subdivision of a Course Offering. Can be used for different cohorts, scheduled runs, or organizations taking the same course. Not required — especially for self-paced evergreen content.

**At MLRI:** Whether to use Sections vs. separate Offerings for cohorts is still an [open decision](../decisions/decision-log.md#sections-vs-new-offerings-for-cohorts).

---

### Program
A named grouping of related Course Templates. For example, *Housing Law* is a Program that contains multiple course templates related to housing and eviction law.

**At MLRI:** Programs live inside Training Areas. A learner might be "in the Housing Law program," meaning they're working through courses in that cluster.

---

### Training Area
A broad content category that groups related Programs. Currently: **Legal Skills** and **Substantive Law**. Helps learners navigate the catalog.

**At MLRI:** The layer below Massachusetts in the hierarchy, above Programs. See [Training Areas](../org-structure/training-areas.md) for full definitions.

---

### Org (Organization)
The top-level entity in Brightspace. Everything lives under the Org. At MLRI, the Org is MLRI itself.

---

### Discover
Brightspace's built-in course catalog/discovery page. Learners can browse and self-enroll in courses through Discover. Whether MLRI uses the built-in Discover page or a custom frontend catalog is an [open decision](../decisions/decision-log.md#discover-page-vs-custom-catalog).

---

### Completion
A record in Brightspace that a learner has finished a course (or met whatever completion criteria were set). Completions are tied to the Course Offering and the learner account — this is why we deactivate accounts rather than deleting them.

---

### Enrollment
The connection between a learner account and a Course Offering. A learner can be enrolled manually by an admin, bulk-imported via CSV, or self-enrolled through Discover.

---

## MLRI-Specific Terms

### Evergreen Course
A course designed to stay live and be updated over time, rather than being run once and closed. Most MLRI courses are evergreen — attorneys may revisit them as the law changes or as they need a refresher.

---

### Major Update
A change to a course that meaningfully affects what it teaches — typically triggered by a law change, substantial content replacement, or new completion requirements. Requires creating a new Course Offering and archiving the old one. See [Versioning & Archiving](../policies/versioning.md).

---

### Minor Update
A change to a course that does not affect the learning outcome — typo fixes, broken links, refreshed examples. Can be made directly in the live Offering without creating a new version.

---

### Archived Offering
A Course Offering that has been superseded by a newer version. Named `ARCHIVED: [Name] V#` and hidden from learners. Preserved so completion records remain intact.
