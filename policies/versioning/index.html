    Versioning & Archiving Policy¶

    Because MLRI courses are evergreen — meaning learners may revisit them over time and content may change as laws or best practices evolve — we need a clear policy for when to update a course in place vs. when to create a new version.

    This policy is not fully decided yet

    The threshold between "minor" and "major" updates is still an open question. The framework below reflects our working understanding. When this is finalized, update this page and mark the decision in the Decision Log.

    The Core Distinction¶

    Update Type What to do
    Minor Edit the existing live course
    Major Create a new Course Offering, archive the old one

    Minor Updates — Edit in Place¶

    A minor update is a change that does not affect the learning outcome or require learners to retake the course.

    Examples of minor updates: - Fixing typos or grammatical errors - Updating broken links - Refreshing examples without changing the substance - Clarifying confusing wording - Adding a note or callout for context - Updating formatting or visuals

    What to do: Make the edit directly in the live Course Offering. No version increment, no archive needed.

    Major Updates — Create a New Version¶

    A major update is a change that meaningfully affects what the course teaches — either because the law changed, the instructional approach changed, or the content was substantially replaced.

    Examples of major updates: - A law, regulation, or court decision changes what attorneys need to know - Substantial content replacement (more than ~30–40% of a module rewritten) - New required material added that all learners should complete - New assessments or completion requirements - A fundamentally different teaching approach for the same topic

    What to do: 1. Create a new Course Offering under the same Course Template 2. Name it with a clean title (current version gets the clean name, old gets ARCHIVED: prefix) 3. Hide the old offering from learners so they can't accidentally enroll 4. Decide whether existing enrolled learners need to take the new version (see Learner Update Notices)

    Archiving Checklist¶

    When creating a new version:

    • New offering created under the correct Course Template
    • Old offering renamed: ARCHIVED: [Name] V#
    • Old offering hidden from learner view
    • New offering is the clean current name
    • Decision documented in the Decision Log (what changed and why)
    • Learner notification decision made (see Learner Update Notices)

    Why We Don't Delete Old Versions¶

    Archived offerings are kept (hidden, not deleted) for several reasons:

    • Learners who completed the old version have a completion record tied to that offering
    • Compliance or grant reporting may require evidence of what content existed at a point in time
    • It lets us compare old and new content if questions arise
